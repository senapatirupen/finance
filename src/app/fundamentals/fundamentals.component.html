Tax Treatment for Share Market Investment

Income from share market trading and investment is taxed differently based on the type of income (capital gains, dividends, business income). Here’s a detailed breakdown of tax treatment for different types of income:

Dividend Income (Mutual Funds & Stocks)
Tax Treatment Example Calculation:
- Dividends from stocks & mutual funds are taxed as per your slab rate
- TDS of 10% is deducted at source if dividend exceeds ₹5,000 per company/fund in a financial year. 		- If you receive ₹1,00,000 in dividends,
- Tax payable = ₹30,000 (30% slab rate)
- If TDS of ₹10,000 is deducted, you must pay ₹20,000 more while filing ITR.
Capital Gains Tax (Stocks & Mutual Funds)
Tax Treatment Example Calculation:
- Short-Term Capital Gain (STCG): Returns are taxed at 20%, if you redeem before one year.
- Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG): After 1 year, you are required to pay LTCG tax of 12.5% on returns of Rs 1.25 lakh+ in a financial year. 		- If you bought stocks for ₹5,00,000 and sold them for ₹6,00,000 after 8 months → ₹1,00,000 STCG @ 20% = ₹20,000 tax.
- If you bought stocks for ₹5,00,000 and sold them for ₹7,50,000 after 2 years: ₹2,50,000 LTCG
First ₹1,20,000 is tax-free, remaining ₹1,30,000 taxed @12.5% = ₹16,250 tax.
Debt Mutual Funds (Less than 65% in equities)
Tax Treatment Example Calculation:
- Budget 2023 Update: No indexation benefit anymore; gains are now taxed at slab rates.
- Both short-term & long-term capital gains are taxed at 30% in your case. 		- Bought debt mutual fund for ₹5,00,000 and sold for ₹5,50,000 after 3 years → ₹50,000 taxed at 30% = ₹15,000.
Speculative Business Income (Intraday Equity Trading)
Tax Treatment Example Calculation:
- Definition: Income from Intraday trading in the equity (cash) market, where no delivery of shares happens. - Example: Buying and selling HDFC Bank shares on the same day.
- Classified as "Business Income" (not capital gains).
- Taxed as per your slab rate.
- Expenses like brokerage, software, internet, and advisory fees can be deducted. - Losses can be set off only against other business income (not salary or capital gains) and carried forward for 4 years. 		- Intraday Profit = ₹5,00,000
- Expenses (Brokerage, Internet, Research Fees, etc.) = ₹50,000
- Net Taxable Income = ₹4,50,000
- Tax Payable (30%) = ₹1,35,000 + 4% cess = ₹1,40,400
Non-Speculative Business Income (F&O Trading)
Tax Treatment Example Calculation:
- Definition: Income from Futures & Options (F&O) trading in stocks, commodities, and currencies. - Example: Buying a NIFTY 50 futures contract and selling it a week later.
- Classified as "Business Income".
- Taxed as per your slab rate.
- Expenses like brokerage, internet, advisory fees, and office expenses are deductible. - Losses can be set off against any other income (except salary) and carried forward for 8 years. 		- F&O Profit = ₹10,00,000
- Expenses (Brokerage, Internet, Research Fees, etc.) = ₹1,00,000
- Net Taxable Income = ₹9,00,000
- Tax Payable (30%) = ₹2,70,000 + 4% cess = ₹2,80,800
Tax Filing Considerations
Audit Required? Advance Tax Payment
- If total turnover (sum of profits & losses) exceeds ₹10 crore, tax audit is mandatory. - Since business income is taxable at slab rates, advance tax must be paid quarterly if total tax liability exceeds ₹10,000 per year.
If turnover is less than ₹2 crore, and you opt for presumptive taxation (44AD), audit is not required unless profit is less than 6% of turnover.

HRA Section 10(13A)

House Rent Allowance (HRA) is a component of a salaried employee’s salary that helps reduce taxable income if they live in a rented house. The tax exemption on HRA is available under Section 10(13A) of the Income Tax Act.

The HRA exemption is the least of the following three amounts:

Actual HRA received from the employer.

50% of basic salary + DA (if living in a metro city: Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata, Chennai) or 40% for non-metro cities.

Rent paid – 10% of basic salary + DA.
